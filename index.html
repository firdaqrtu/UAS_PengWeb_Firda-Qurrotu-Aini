WELCOME TO THE TOGAWA LIBRARY

Togawa Library is a modern public library dedicated to learning, creativity, and community. Brought to you by Togawa Group, we strive to provide an accessible and inspiring space where everyone can explore knowledge, discover new interests, and connect with one another.

NEW COLLECTIONS

Kokoro by Natsume Sōseki

Kokoro—"the heart of things"—is the work of one of Japan's most popular authors. This thought-provoking trilogy of stories explores the very essence of loneliness and stands as a stirring introduction to modern Japanese literature.

View Details

Demian by Hermann Hesse

Demian is a coming-of-age novel about Emil Sinclair's spiritual journey from a sheltered, innocent childhood into self-discovery, guided by the enigmatic Max Demian, who introduces him to a world beyond conventional morality, blending light and darkness to find wholeness, a process symbolized by the "mark of Cain" and the Gnostic deity Abraxas.

View Details

Ash Wednesday by T.S. Eliot

This book deals with the struggle that ensues when one who has lacked faith in the past strives to move towards God. In contemporary circles, it is often read published within The Waste Land and Other Poems.

View Details
